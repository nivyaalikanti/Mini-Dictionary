Cacophony

Definition

A harsh, discordant mixture of sounds.

Use Cases

  • The old factory produced a cacophony of clanging metal and hissing steam.
  • The orchestra's warm-up session was a cacophony of tuning instruments before the conductor arrived.
  • During rush hour, the city streets are filled with a cacophony of car horns, sirens, and shouting.
  • The kindergarten classroom, just before dismissal, erupted into a cacophony of children's voices.
  • He tried to concentrate, but the cacophony from the construction site next door made it impossible.
Synonyms
Discord Din Racket
Noise Dissonance Clamor
Antonyms
Harmony Melody Silence
Euphony Concord Quiet
