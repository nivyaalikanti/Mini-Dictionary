Defenestrate

Definition

To throw (someone or something) out of a window.

Use Cases

  • In a fit of rage, he threatened to defenestrate the broken television.
  • Historically, acts of defenestration have sometimes been used as a form of protest or assassination.
  • The old building's windows were so fragile, you could almost imagine someone accidentally defenestrating a book.
  • The crowd cheered when the fictional villain was defenestrated from the tower in the movie.
  • While highly unlikely, the cartoon character managed to defenestrate an entire piano.
